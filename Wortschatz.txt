In most situations, especially in spoken language, I don't think there really is any. But I think "Ich merkte" means I realize something while "Ich bemerkte" means I perceive something, thus merken involves thinking about it and arriving at some conclusion, unlike bemerken.<br><br><b>bemerken</b> also means to remark/to point out/to observe:<br>EJ: Darf ich nur (ein)mal bemerken, dass ... = May I just remark / point out that ...<br>EJ: "...," bemerkte er trocken. = "...," he observed drily.<br><br><b>anmerken</b> means to notice/observe something about a person, usually about their emotional state or situation, that s/he isn't showing openly, possibly even trying to hide.<br><br>EJ: Ich merkte ihm an, dass er nicht bei der Sache war. = I could tell he was distracted. / He seemed distracted.<br>EJ: Man merkte ihr an, dass sie aufgeregt war. = One could tell she was nervous. / She seemed nervous.<br>EJ: Ich merkte ihr an, dass sie lieber woanders wäre. = I could tell she'd rather be someplace else.<br>EJ: Ich merkte ihm an, dass er Besseres gewohnt war. = I could tell that he was used to something better.<br><br><b>anmerken</b> also means to remark / to note / to point out (same as bemerken)<br>EJ: Ich möchte nur einmal anmerken, dass ... = I'd like to note / point out<br>EJ: Dazu möchte ich noch anmerken, dass ... = On this matter, I'd also like to point outIn both sentences, you could also use bemerken.<br><br><b>vermerken</b> is formal and means (in less formal language) notieren or eintragen.<br>EJ: Ich vermerkte (notierte) den Termin in meinem Kalender.<br>EJ: Ich vermerkte auf allen Rechnungen/in jedem Formular ...
